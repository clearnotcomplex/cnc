The Four Stoic Virtues

Philosophy • January 2025

A visual guide to the four cardinal virtues of Stoic philosophy. The Stoics believed that living according to these virtues was the path to a good life—what they called eudaimonia.

Wisdom (Sophia) — The ability to navigate complex situations in a logical, informed, and calm manner. It's about making good judgments and understanding what truly matters.

Courage (Andreia) — Not just physical bravery, but moral courage. The strength to do what's right, face adversity, and act on your convictions even when difficult.

Justice (Dikaiosyne) — Treating others fairly and with respect. It encompasses integrity, honesty, and contributing positively to your community.

Temperance (Sophrosyne) — Self-control and moderation in all things. The discipline to resist excess and maintain balance in thoughts, actions, and emotions.
